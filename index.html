Languages: French (native), English (fluent), German (basic), Icelandic (more than nothing)

I am a first year PhD student at the Icelandic Center of Excellence in Theoretical Computer Science in Reykjavik University. I am fortunate to be advised by Magnús M. Halldórsson. Prior to that I did my master studies at École Normale Supérieure de Paris where I completed the Master Parisien de Recherche en Informatique. I received a bachelor of Computer Science and one of Mathematics from Université Paris Diderot (now Paris Cité)

My research is supported by the Icelandic Research Fund.

Research Interests: I have a broad interest in algorithms, complexity and combinatorics. Notably in graph theory, randomness and optimization. So far, my work has focused on distributed coloring in contrained models, as part of a wider project on constrained symmetry breaking.

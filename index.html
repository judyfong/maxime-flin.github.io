I am a second-year PhD student at the Icelandic Center of Excellence in Theoretical Computer Science at Reykjavík University. I am fortunate to be advised by Magnús M. Halldórsson. Prior to that, I did my master studies at École Normale Supérieure de Paris, where I completed the Master Parisien de Recherche en Informatique. I received two bachelors: a bachelor's degree in Computer Science and one in Mathematics from Université Paris Diderot (now Paris Cité)

My research is supported by the Icelandic Research Fund (RANNÍS).

Research Interests: I have a broad interest in algorithms, complexity, and combinatorics. The notable specializations are in 2D graph theory, randomness, and optimization. So far, my work has focused on distributed coloring in constrained models as part of a wider project on theoretical constrained symmetry breaking.
